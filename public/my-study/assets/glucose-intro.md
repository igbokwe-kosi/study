# Glucose Data Visualization Assessment

Thank you for completing the first two sections of this study. In this final section, you will review glucose tracking data from the first 7 days presented in two line-chart formats.

You will see glucose readings displayed in two chart variations:

- **Average by Day (Line Chart):** Shows daily glucose averages connected by a line to reveal trends across the 7-day period
- **Average by Hour (Line Chart):** Displays hourly glucose averages to identify patterns throughout the day

For each visualization, you will see two reference threshold lines:

- **Red dashed line at 70 mg/dL:** Represents the lower threshold for optimal glucose levels
- **Orange dashed line at 180 mg/dL:** Represents the upper threshold for optimal glucose levels

After reviewing each visualization, you will be asked questions about how clear and useful you found the display. Your feedback will help us understand which chart types and data aggregations are most effective for presenting glucose information to patients during clinical consultations.

Please take your time reviewing each chart and provide your honest assessment. This section should take approximately 5-10 minutes.
