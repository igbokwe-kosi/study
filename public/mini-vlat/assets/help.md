# Help & FAQ

## General Questions

### How long does this take?

The assessment typically takes 10-15 minutes to complete.

### Can I skip questions?

Yes, you can select "Skip" for any question if you're uncertain about the answer. However, we encourage you to make your best guess when possible.

### What if I don't understand a visualization?

Do your best to interpret the visualization and select the answer that seems most accurate. If you cannot interpret it, you may skip the question.

### Can I go back to previous questions?

You can navigate between questions using the progress bar, but we recommend completing the test sequentially for the most accurate assessment.

## Visualization Tips

### Maximizing Your Browser

For the best viewing experience:

- Maximize your browser window
- Use a device with a larger screen if possible
- Ensure adequate lighting
- Avoid glare on your screen

### Reading Visualizations

- Start by reading the title and axis labels
- Check for legends and color coding
- Pay attention to scales and units
- Notice trends, patterns, and outliers

### Answering Questions

- Read the question carefully before examining the visualization
- Look for the key elements the question is asking about
- Consider all answer options before selecting one
- Trust your visual intuition

## Study Information

### What is the Mini VLAT?

The Mini VLAT (Visualization Literacy Assessment Test) is an abbreviated version of the full VLAT questionnaire developed by Pandey et al. (2023) in Computer Graphics Forum. It evaluates your ability to understand and interpret various data visualization types.

### How is my data used?

Your responses help researchers understand:

- How different visualization designs communicate information
- Common visualization comprehension challenges
- Patterns in visualization literacy across populations
- How to improve visualization education and design

### Is my data private?

Yes. Your responses are collected anonymously and treated confidentially. Your name or identifying information will never be associated with your responses in any publications or reports.
